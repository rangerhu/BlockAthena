KuCoin Hack Summary

On September 25, 2020, the centralized cryptocurrency exchange KuCoin experienced a major security breach involving the compromise of its hot wallets.

Attack Time:
- UTC: 2020-09-25 23:05 (approx)
- Ethereum Block Height: ~10,820,000

Attack Details:
- Private keys to KuCoin's hot wallets were stolen.
- The attackers drained over $280 million worth of assets from Ethereum, Bitcoin, and other blockchains.
- Ethereum-based tokens involved include USDT, USDC, LINK, SNX, and others.

Modus Operandi:
- Funds were transferred in large batches from KuCoin hot wallets to attacker-controlled addresses.
- Attackers used DEXs such as Uniswap and Curve to swap and mix the stolen tokens.
- Part of the funds were laundered via cross-chain protocols to obfuscate traceability.

Notable Characteristics:
- Highly synchronized token draining.
- Use of bot-controlled wallets and frequent swaps within short intervals.
- Strong co-occurrence behavior patterns were observed among attacker addresses.
